Our History

P&P was established by two German siblings whose grandparents migrated
to Malaysia after World War I, bringing with them not only their skills but
also a deep appreciation for craftsmanship and tradition. The family initially
built their livelihood around a humble blacksmith shop, where precision,
patience, and hard work shaped every piece they created. For decades, the
shop served the local community, becoming a quiet symbol of reliability
and heritage. However, as modern mechanical technologies began to advance,
the demand for traditional forging gradually declined. Tools and machinery
replaced handcrafted work, and the once-thriving trade slowly lost its
place in a rapidly changing world. Rather than holding on to the
past, the siblings chose to embrace change while preserving their roots.
Inspired by their family’s legacy, they reimagined the space—not as a
workshop of metal, but as a place of warmth and connection. On May 4, 1993,
the blacksmith shop was officially transformed into a café, marking the
beginning of a new chapter. Today, P&P stands as more than just a café; it
is a blend of history and modernity, where the spirit of craftsmanship lives
on through carefully crafted drinks, comforting food, and an inviting atmosphere
that welcomes all.
